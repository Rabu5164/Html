The Six-Point Movement, also known as the Six-Point Demand, was a political movement in East Pakistan (now Bangladesh) in the late 1960s. Led by Sheikh Mujibur Rahman and the Awami League, it demanded greater autonomy and economic rights for East Pakistan within a federal framework.
